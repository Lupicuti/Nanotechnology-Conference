Vision & Scope

Bridging Nanotechnology, Materials Science & Medical Innovation

Targeting Bachelor's students, Master’s students, PhD candidates, and active researchers, this conference bridges high-level theoretical discoveries and transformative real-world applications across physics, chemistry, and engineering.

Our distinguished keynotes explore the frontiers of modern nanoscience: from sub-Angstrom single-atom imaging (Prof. Haider & Prof. Suenaga) and topological quantum computation with Majorana fermions (Prof. Beenakker), to CVD graphene & terahertz polaritonics (Prof. Coletti & Prof. Tredicucci), 3D plasmonic neuro-biosensors (Prof. De Angelis), and eco-friendly nanorestoration of cultural heritage (Prof. Di Carlo).

2D Materials & Graphene Aberration-Corrected Microscopy Terahertz & Quantum Optics Topological Superconductors Reticular Chemistry (MOFs) Nanofluidics & Nanomedicine Cultural Heritage Conservation

Call for Abstracts • PhDs & Young Researchers

Scientific Poster Session

Present your cutting-edge research before world-renowned keynote speakers, senior faculty, and international peers at Sapienza University of Rome.

Why Submit Your Research?

The SSAS Conference Poster Session is specifically designed to give PhD students, postdocs, and early-career investigators an active platform to showcase their breakthroughs, receive constructive feedback, and establish collaborations across nanoscience and advanced engineering.

Direct Scientific Networking

Engage directly during the dedicated coffee & networking sessions with plenary speakers including Prof. Beenakker, Prof. Suenaga, Prof. Haider, and top Italian researchers.

Peer Discussion & Visibility

Present your research methodology and preliminary or published findings directly to the interdisciplinary academic community and conference attendees during open gallery sessions.

Format & Technical Setup

Posters should be printed in standard vertical A0 format (84.1 × 118.9 cm). Display stands, mounting panels, and materials will be provided on-site at the Aula Amaldi foyer.

Abstract Submission Deadline: July 15, 2026
Early submissions are recommended due to limited exhibition panel slots.
